Event survey importance

Exit interview surveys are a powerful tool for retention and improvement in organizations.
They can help you understand why employees stay, why they leave, and how the organization needs to change1.
They can also reveal hidden challenges and opportunities, generate competitive intelligence, and promote engagement and appreciation.
Exit interview surveys can help you identify potential retention issues before they lead to people leaving, and analyze trends and patterns by demographic, tenure, performance, and roles.
They can also turn departing employees into corporate ambassadors for years to come.

Event survey question examples

How did you hear about this event?
What were your main goals for attending this event?
How satisfied are you with the event overall?
How likely are you to recommend this event to a friend or colleague?
What did you like most about this event?
What did you like least about this event?
How would you rate the quality of the speakers, sessions, venue, food, etc.?
How relevant and useful was the content of this event for you?
What topics or speakers would you like to see at future events?
Do you have any suggestions for improving this event?

Importance of event surveys

Event surveys are important because they help you evaluate the success of your event and improve it for the future. By collecting feedback from your attendees and other stakeholders, you can:

Generate more leads by understanding the needs and challenges of your target market.
Win more marketing budget by showing the return on investment and impact of your event.
Understand your audience more, including their preferences, expectations, and satisfaction levels.
Collect social proof and other data points for future marketing efforts.
Build trust and loyalty by showing that you care about your attendees’ opinions and experiences.
Eliminate the guesswork and make data-driven decisions based on actual feedback.
Estimate event growth by measuring the net promoter score and word-of-mouth potential of your event.
